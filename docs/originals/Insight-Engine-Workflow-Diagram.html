How the Insight Engine works
the /analyse runtime — what happens when you hand it a problem
You · /analyse "<your problem>"
▼
⚑ Proportionality triage
Unambiguously trivial, self-contained, reversible, no third parties or legal exposure?
YES (rare) → a short, proportionate answer — and an offer to run the full pass
NO / any doubt → full depth (the default)
▼
STEP 0
Optional scoping
a few skippable questions — the decision, the audience, the value frame, any documents you hold
▼
STEP 1
Provocation pass
surface what's material but sits where attention doesn't go; mark each claim [V] or [N]
▼
STEP 2
Verification
route each claim · tier it [V1]/[V2]/[V3] · search the negation of the load-bearing few · re-scan for what focus dropped
▼
STEP 2.5
Systems-investigation pass · conditional
only when the problem is genuinely systemic (feedback, stocks, delays, possible tipping): map a graded causal structure — loops, tipping conditions, leverage. Investigate, don't predict.
▼
STEP 3
Deep-core routing
set aside the questions no evidence can settle: framing · legitimacy · opportunity-cost · value
▼
STEP 4
Grade-lock + resilience check
fix every grade; stress-test whether the call survives its load-bearing assumptions moving together
▼
STEP 5
★ THE FORCED GATE YOU
you take a one-line position on each deep-core question — or explicitly defer.
The brief does not finalise until you do.
▼
STEP 6
Assemble → the decision brief
your positions · graded findings · call · calibration · dominant unknown · what-to-verify-first · what-would-flip-it · sources
